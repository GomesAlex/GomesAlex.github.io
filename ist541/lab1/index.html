How to Fill a Fountain Pen

By Alex Gomes


Step 1: Gather Items

If you haven’t tried writing with a fountain pen before, you should consider it. Being able to write without placing pressure on the paper allows for a luxurious writing experience. Thousands of different colored inks are available to try. The pens are refillable, so you don’t have to throw them away. Additionally, many offer different shaped nibs (the part of the pen that meets the paper) allowing for expressive writing that a standard ball point can’t offer.

To get started we will need several items to complete this process:

  • Cartridge converter fountain pen
  • Bottle of ink
  • Old towel or paper towels
Step 2a: Disassemble the Pen - Major Parts

Although there are many different filling systems for a fountain pen, this tutorial will cover one of the most common known as a cartridge converter. This style allows for easy refilling without needing any special tools.

Step 2b: Disassemble the Pen - Dissasembly

Most pens are similar. If you are unsure how to disassemble, please review the manufacturer information. For the purposes of this tutorial, unscrew the barrel of the pen while holding the section.

Step 2c: Disassemble the Pen - Filling System

You will now see the converter mechanism. Make sure there isn’t any ink in the pen. Turn the top knob on the converter to see how the mechanism works. Notice the piston inside of the converter moves up and down. This is what creates the suction to pull in the ink. Proceed to the next step.

Step 3: Prepare the Bottle

While it is not difficult to open a bottle, it is difficult to clean up ink which may permanently dye surfaces if spilled. To prevent any possible frustrations, it is important to pick a uncluttered surface that is easily cleanable such as a kitchen counter. To protect the surface, place a towel under the ink bottle. Uncap the bottle only when ready to fill the pen.

Step 4: Fill the Pen

Insert the nib of the pen below the ink level. Turn the converter now so that the piston is retracting and pulling ink inside the converter chamber. Once the converter knob stops turning, you are done. Set the pen down onto a towel.

Step 5: Cap the Bottle

Immediately cap the ink bottle to prevent spills! It is easy to forget or become overly excited and forget this critical step and then accidentally bump the bottle.

Step 6: Clean and Assemble the Pen

Wipe the excess ink from the nib with a towel so it doesn’t drip or pool on the paper. Proceed to assemble the pen by placing the barrel of the pen and screwing back onto the section. It is important to note that you don’t have to screw tight with excessive force. To ensure your pen lasts a lifetime tighten up to the point the parts stop.

Step 7: Write On!

The pen is ready to go, and it is time to start writing. Enjoy!
